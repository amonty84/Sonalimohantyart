I think I spent most of my engineering days painting or thinking about painting. But as in most cases, life took over and I lost touch with the brush.So, when I became a mother, my life changed in more ways than I imagined. I rediscovered my love for painting and soon I was back on the drawing board at every possible chance. I got my first break by an art gallery set up by an old friend, and she helped me understand what it means to be a professional artist. I exhibited in various restaurants, shows and events and was also featured in the upcoming artist section in Better Interior Magazine in November 2010. My painting career took off slowly and I got a lot of traction through my Facebook art page.  My career got its biggest boost when an established art gallery in Singapore decided to take a chance on a self-taught artist.I don’t know if it would have helped me as an artist to get a formal education, but the process of learning makes me very excited and keeps me motivated. I experiment a lot with textures and techniques and everything around me inspires me to interpret it in my own unique way. I totally believe in the words by Ella Fitzgerald “Just don’t give up trying to do what you really want to do. Where there is love and inspiration, I don’t think you can go wrong.” I love what I do, and every new painting inspires me to learn and do better.
